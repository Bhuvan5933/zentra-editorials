Tourism and Sustainable Development in Kashmir: A Multi-pronged Approach

T he April 2025 Pahalgam attack forced India to confront the fact that tourism recovers only when visitors can predict what will happen to them and local communities see credible bene ts from keeping the sites open. Following the attack, the local administration closed 48 government-approved tourist sites, later reopening them in phases, including 14 on February 16. Tourists have rated the Kashmir Valley as relatively safe overall while still differentiating types of risk, which means that tourism policy in the region needs to focus on what visitors can be certain about and whether the state has a fixed and lucid rationale for closing or reopening sites. In the Union Budget 2026-27 announcement, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman described a two-pronged plan to enhance tourism: institutional capacity building and developing trails and heritage sites. She also singled out the development of ecologically sustainable mountain trails in Jammu and Kashmir, which is good because formal trails admit better management, including ticketing, permits, deployment of rangers, and medical facilities, and reduce fragility by diversifying the ‘Kashmir experience’. Kashmir could also do with a third prong: it is a biodiverse region that has become heavily militarised and shared environmental governance can help build trust. The central government should consider paid civic roles rather than relying on only volunteer awareness campaigns, using protocols that forest protection committees already implement around protected areas. These can include trail maintenance, waste management, guiding, fire watch, and (wildlife) conflict mitigation. An influx of tourists can jolt the local economy to provide more and better services, so that over time, more families are incentivised to speak out against terrorism that suppresses tourism. Functional sites also need clear rules, reliable permits, fast help during emergencies, working roads, clean public spaces, and good communication, and the lower disruption is the state’s incentive to solve these problems. Tourism can also help reduce fear and isolation by bringing into local contact people from across India and fostering business ties. Tourism and allied services can give young people a real path into the economy by skilling or reskilling them. The people of the region deserve more civilian ownership of social stability and more negotiating power, especially one that outstrips their cause for resentment.

A. ERROR SPOTTING - TYPE 1

Q1. In each of the following questions, five sentences are given, out of which one or more may contain a grammatical or contextual error. You are required to identify the sentence(s) that contains the grammatical or contextual error.

A) The success of any tourism initiative

B) largely depend on the local community's support

C) and the perception of safety

D) among potential visitors.

E) No error.

ANSWER: B

EXPLANATION:

The subject of the sentence is 'The success', which is singular. Therefore, the verb should be singular 'depends' instead of 'depend'. This is a subject-verb agreement error.

Correct Sentence: The success of any tourism initiative largely depends on the local community's support and the perception of safety among potential visitors.

Q2. In each of the following questions, five sentences are given, out of which one or more may contain a grammatical or contextual error. You are required to identify the sentence(s) that contains the grammatical or contextual error.

A) Developing ecologically sustainable trails

B) not only enhances visitor experience

C) but also reduce the fragility

D) of the regional ecosystem.

E) No error.

ANSWER: C

EXPLANATION:

The phrase 'not only... but also' requires parallel structure. If 'enhances' (singular verb) is used after 'not only', then 'reduce' should also be 'reduces' (singular verb) to match the singular subject 'developing'.

Correct Sentence: Developing ecologically sustainable trails not only enhances visitor experience but also reduces the fragility of the regional ecosystem.

Q3. In each of the following questions, five sentences are given, out of which one or more may contain a grammatical or contextual error. You are required to identify the sentence(s) that contains the grammatical or contextual error.

A) The government is considering

B) to implement paid civic roles

C) for local residents

D) to manage tourist sites.

E) No error.

ANSWER: B

EXPLANATION:

The verb 'consider' is typically followed by a gerund (V-ing) when referring to a future action, not an infinitive. So, 'considering implementing' is correct.

Correct Sentence: The government is considering implementing paid civic roles for local residents to manage tourist sites.

Q11. In the following questions, five sentences are given, out of which one may or may not contain a grammatical or contextual error. You are required to identify the sentence that is grammatically and contextually correct.

A) The government considers to introduce new policies.

B) The government considers introducing new policies.

C) The government considering to introduce new policies.

D) The government considering introducing new policies.

E) The government consider introducing new policies.

ANSWER: B

EXPLANATION:

The verb 'consider' is typically followed by a gerund (the -ing form of a verb) when it refers to thinking about a future action or possibility. Therefore, 'considering introducing' is the correct construction. Option B correctly uses this form. Options A and C use the infinitive 'to introduce' incorrectly after 'consider' or an incomplete verb 'considering'. Option D is missing the auxiliary verb 'is' before 'considering'. Option E has a subject-verb agreement error ('government' is singular, so it should be 'considers').

Q12. In the following questions, five sentences are given, out of which one may or may not contain a grammatical or contextual error. You are required to identify the sentence that is grammatically and contextually correct.

A) Neither the local administration nor the central government has addressed all concerns.

B) Neither the local administration nor the central government have addressed all concerns.

C) Neither the local administration or the central government has addressed all concerns.

D) Neither the local administration nor the central government addressed all concerns.

E) Neither the local administration nor the central government have been addressed all concerns.

ANSWER: A

EXPLANATION:

When using 'neither... nor', the verb agrees with the noun closest to it. In this case, 'central government' is singular, so the verb 'has' (singular) is correct. Option A correctly uses 'neither... nor' and the singular verb 'has'. Option B uses 'have', which is plural. Option C incorrectly uses 'or' with 'neither'. Option D is grammatically correct but changes the tense from perfect to simple past, which might alter the nuance; 'has addressed' is usually preferred for an action whose effect continues. Option E uses 'have been addressed', which implies the government itself was addressed, changing the meaning and making it grammatically incorrect in context.

Q13. In the following questions, five sentences are given, out of which one may or may not contain a grammatical or contextual error. You are required to identify the sentence that is grammatically and contextually correct.

A) To reduce fragility, the region must diversifying its tourism offerings.

B) To reduce fragility, the region must diversify its tourism offerings.

C) For reducing fragility, the region must diversify its tourism offerings.

D) To reduce fragility, the region needs to diversifying its tourism offerings.

E) To reduce fragility, the region must have diversified its tourism offerings.

ANSWER: B

EXPLANATION:

Modal verbs like 'must' are always followed by the base form of the verb (infinitive without 'to'). Therefore, 'must diversify' is the correct construction. Option B follows this rule. Option A incorrectly uses 'diversifying'. Option C uses 'For reducing', which is grammatically less precise than 'To reduce' for expressing purpose. Option D incorrectly uses 'needs to diversifying'. Option E uses 'must have diversified', which implies a past action that was required, not a present necessity for a future action.

Q14. In the following questions, five sentences are given, out of which one may or may not contain a grammatical or contextual error. You are required to identify the sentence that is grammatically and contextually correct.

D) Local communities is incentivised to speak out against terrorism.

The sentence requires a passive voice construction because the communities are being motivated or encouraged ('incentivised') by something external. Option C is active voice and lacks the auxiliary verb. Option D has a subject-verb agreement error ('communities' is plural, so 'is' is incorrect). Option E uses 'for speaking out' which is acceptable but 'to speak out' is more direct for purpose here and matches the common phrasing from the editorial.

Q16. The government aims to ________ institutional capacity for better disaster management.

EXPLANATION:

The sentence suggests a positive action towards capacity. 'Enhance' means to intensify, increase, or further improve, which fits the context of building institutional capacity. 'Diminish', 'retract', 'curtail', and 'restrict' all imply reducing or limiting, which is the opposite of the intended meaning.

  • retract: To draw back or in; to withdraw (a statement or accusation).

  • restrict: To put a limit on; keep under control.

Q17. The lack of a ________ rationale for site closures caused public confusion.

A) vagueB) obscureC) lucidD) ambiguousE) unclear

ANSWER: C

EXPLANATION:

The sentence implies that the absence of a clear reason caused confusion. 'Lucid' means expressed clearly; easy to understand. This directly contrasts with 'public confusion'. The other options ('vague', 'obscure', 'ambiguous', 'unclear') are synonyms for 'unclear' and would logically lead to confusion, making 'lack of' followed by one of these words redundant or grammatically awkward. Thus, 'lack of a lucid rationale' fits perfectly.

Word Meanings:

  • vague: Of uncertain, indefinite, or unclear character or meaning.
  • obscure: Not discovered or known about; uncertain.
  • lucid: Expressed clearly; easy to understand.
  • ambiguous: Open to more than one interpretation; having a double meaning.
  • unclear: Not clear or easy to understand.

  • jolted: Pushed or shook abruptly; gave a sudden shock or surprise to.
  • boosted: Helped to improve or increase (something).
